Introduction — Slide 1 of 8

What is a Battery Management System?

A Battery Management System (BMS) is an electronic system built into rechargeable battery packs. Its job is to monitor and control the battery to ensure it operates safely at all times.

🔍

Monitors

Tracks voltage, temperature, and current in real time

🛡️

Protects

Prevents overcharge, over-discharge, and short circuits

⚡

Optimizes

Balances cells to maximize performance and lifespan

🤖

Automates

Stops charging, shuts off device — all automatically

💡

Think of it this way

The BMS is the "safety manager" of the battery. Without it, lithium batteries could overheat, overcharge, or become unstable.

⚠️

Key agent insight

Some behaviors customers think are defects are actually the BMS working correctly — auto charge-stop, sudden shutdown at low battery, and charge slowdown near full capacity are all normal BMS behaviors.

Key Functions — Slide 3 of 8

5 Core BMS Functions

  • 1

    Cell Monitoring — Checks each cell's voltage continuously to keep all cells within safe operating ranges.

  • 2

    Temperature Monitoring — Stops charging or discharge if the battery gets too hot or too cold. This is why products may not charge in extreme temperatures.

  • 3

    Overcharge Protection — Once the battery reaches 100%, the BMS stops charging automatically. Charger light turning green = BMS has stopped — not a defect.

  • 4

    Over-Discharge Protection — If battery voltage drops dangerously low, the BMS shuts off the product to prevent permanent cell damage.

  • 5

    Short Circuit Protection — Immediately cuts all power if an electrical fault is detected.

Charge Cycles — Slide 4 of 8

What is a Charge Cycle?

A charge cycle = one complete use of the battery's total capacity — from full to empty and back to full.

📊

Partial uses count too

Using 50% battery one day + 50% the next day = 1 full charge cycle. This applies to both Jetson and Shop Joe products.

300–500
Approximate charge cycles for most lithium batteries

Actual lifespan depends on usage habits, charging behavior, and environmental conditions. This is why proper charging practices matter for battery longevity.

Charging FAQs — Slide 5 of 8

Charging Best Practices

  • Q

    Can I charge overnight?
    Not recommended. Leaving the product plugged in overnight can diminish battery life over time. Always refer customers to their user manual for the recommended charge time and method.

  • Q

    How long should charging take?
    Charging time varies by product. Never quote a universal time. Always direct customers to their user manual.

  • Q

    Should I drain the battery fully before charging?
    No. Lithium batteries actually perform better with partial charging cycles. Full depletion repeatedly can stress cells.

📋

Agent Reminder

Always guide customers back to manufacturer recommendations in the user manual. Don't speculate on specific charge times.

BMS Symptoms — Slide 6 of 8

Common BMS-Related Symptoms

When customers describe these issues, recognize the underlying BMS behavior:

Customer SaysBMS Cause
"Why won't my battery charge?" BMS lockout / over-discharge protection — battery too depleted, or charge protection triggered
"Why did my product shut off suddenly?" BMS over-discharge cut-off — battery hit critically low voltage; BMS cut power to protect cells
"Charger light is green but battery isn't full" BMS charge termination — BMS detected full capacity and stopped. This is normal, not a defect.
"Is it safe to leave it plugged in?" No. Do not leave plugged in beyond the recommended charge time listed in the product manual
End-of-Life & Escalation — Slide 7 of 8

Recognizing Battery EOL

SymptomCauseAgent Check
"Used to last an hour, now 10 min" Cell degradation after many cycles Ask purchase date & usage. Gradual = EOL. Sudden = possible BMS fault.
"Battery won't hold charge" EOL capacity loss or BMS triggered Confirm OEM charger. Charge indicator works but drains = likely EOL
"Shows full, shuts off in minutes" Voltage drop under load — classic EOL Gradual or sudden? Strong EOL sign. Escalate if under warranty.
🚨

Escalate Immediately When:

  • Battery overheats (too hot to touch — not normal warmth)
  • Fire is reported
  • Battery swelling or visible damage
🌡️

When customer says "battery feels hot" — ask first:

  • Is it warm, or too hot to touch?
  • During charging or during use?
  • Did the product shut off or show a warning?
Safety & Summary — Slide 8 of 8

Safety Messaging & Key Takeaways

🔒

Always Communicate to Customers

  • Never use damaged chargers
  • Avoid extreme heat or cold
  • Stop using immediately if battery swells
  • Follow manufacturer charging instructions (user manual)
✅

BMS Protects

The BMS protects both the battery and the user — many "issues" are intentional BMS behaviors

🔄

Cycles Matter

300–500 charge cycles is the expected lithium lifespan. Partial uses count.

🚨

Know When to Escalate

Overheating, fire, and swelling = immediate escalation. Warmth = ask first.
